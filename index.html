What is our purpose?

At the core of our non-profit organization, the process of cat adoptions stands as the culmination of our dedicated endeavors. Through relentless rescue efforts, empathetic rehabilitation, and steadfast advocacy, we carve a path for these feline companions to enter a life brimming with affection, coziness, and companionship.

Who are we?

PinkyPaws is more than just an NGO; it's a movement dedicated to championing the rights and well-being of cats. At PinkyPaws, our mission is to create a world where every feline friend is cherished, protected, and provided with the opportunity to live a life filled with love and well-being. Through our commitment to providing loving homes for cats and promoting responsible pet ownership, we aim to enhance the well-being of both cats and their human counterparts.

Adoption Fee

Covering care costs

Adoption fees are essential to cover the expenses associated with caring for animals in shelters, including veterinary care, vaccinations, and other healthcare needs. This financial contribution ensures that animals receive the necessary attention and support during their time in the shelter.

Promoting responsible adoption

The adoption fee serves as a valuable deterrent against impulsive adoptions. By requiring a financial commitment, shelters aim to ensure that individuals adopting pets are genuinely committed to providing a loving and stable home. This helps prevent situations where pets are adopted on a whim, only to be returned later due to unforeseen challenges.

Supporting sustainable rescue efforts

Adoption fees play a pivotal role in sustaining the ongoing efforts of rescue organizations. These fees contribute to the overall funding of the organization, enabling them to rescue, rehabilitate, and care for a greater number of animals. In this way, adoption fees are crucial for the long-term viability of rescue initiatives and the well-being of animals in need.

Most asked questions 

"How long the adoption process lasts?"

The duration of the adoption process can vary depending on several factors, including the specific policies of the adoption agency or shelter, the thoroughness of the application process, and the individual circumstances of both the adopter and the pet. Typically, the process may take anywhere from a few days to a few weeks.

"How often will the control visits be?"

Subsequent control visits might occur periodically over the first several months to track the cat's adjustment, address any behavior issues, and provide support to the adopter. These visits are often beneficial for both the adopted cat and the new owner, fostering a positive relationship and addressing any potential challenges early on.

"Can I return the cat if I don't get along with it?"

Many agencies encourage adopters to give the cat some time to adjust to its new home before considering a return. Additionally, they may offer support and resources to help overcome challenges, such as behavior issues or integration into the household. Some organizations may have a grace period during which adopters can return the cat without repercussions.
